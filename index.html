We are comparing the baseline d-vector speaker embedding with the proposed LA-SE speaker embedding through three case studies for cross-lingual personalized speech generation:
(1) Multi-Task WaveRNN for Cross-Lingual Voice Conversion:
In this case study, there 4 systems for comparison:
(a) d-vector (cross): The Multi-Task WaveRNN framework conditioned on d-vector obtained under the cross-lingual condition.
(b) d-vector (mono): The Multi-Task WaveRNN framework conditioned on d-vector obtained under the monolingual condition.
(c) LA-SE (cross): The Multi-Task WaveRNN framework conditioned on LA-SE obtained under the cross-lingual condition.
(d) LA-SE (mono): The Multi-Task WaveRNN framework conditioned on LA-SE obtained under the monolingual condition.
(2) CycleVAE for Cross-Lingual Voice Conversion:
In this case study, there 2 systems for comparison:
(a) d-vector: The CycleVAE framework conditioned on d-vector obtained under the cross-lingual condition.
(b) LA-SE: The CycleVAE framework conditioned on LA-SE obtained under the cross-lingual condition.
(3) Tacotron-2 for Text-to-Speech Synthesis
In this case study, there 2 systems for comparison:
(a) d-vector: The Tacotron-2 framework conditioned on d-vector obtained under the cross-lingual condition.
(b) LA-SE: The Tacotron-2 framework conditioned on LA-SE obtained under the cross-lingual condition.

Source: Speech from a source speaker, we hope to preserve the speech content.
Target: Speech from a target speaker, we hope the converted speech sound like this speaker's voice.
Reference: Reference speech from the target speaker speaking the source content.
ENG → MAN:
we are converting an English source utterance to a Mandarin target speaker's voice.
MAN → ENG:
we are converting a Mandarin source utterance to an English target speaker's voice.
